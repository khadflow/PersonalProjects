## 🛠️ HOW-TO: Adding Attack Colliders to Character Joints

Follow these steps whenever you need to map a new attack animation to a physical skeletal joint on a character model:

### 1. Hierarchy Setup (The Bone)

* Locate the target bone in the character's skeletal hierarchy (e.g., `mixamorig:RightFoot`).
* Create a **new empty GameObject** as a **direct, immediate child** of that specific bone.
* Name the GameObject identically to your target move or joint convention (e.g., `AttackCollider`).

### 2. Component Configuration

On the new `AttackCollider` GameObject, add the following two components:

* **Collider (Box or Sphere):**
* Check **`Is Trigger = true`**.
* Adjust the size and center offsets in the Scene View so it matches the striking area of the limb.


* **`AttackColliderTrigger` Script:**
* Attach your trigger handling script to this child object.



### 3. State Management Registration

* Open `AttackColliders.cs`.
* Cache the joint bone using `FindBoneUniversal(animator.transform, "TargetBoneName")`.
* Retrieve the collider cleanly using `GetColliderFromTriggerScript(targetBoneBone)`.
* Wire up your Activation/Deactivation methods and update your `EmptyQueue()` execution block in `AttackTrie.cs` to ensure the collider cleanly switches on when the animation plays.
* Handle the timeout in the C_Controller in LateUpdate() for missed attacks
